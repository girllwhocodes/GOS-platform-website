Grants and scholarships

For colleges and universities, scholarships are financial aid awards designed to help students pay for an undergraduate degree. Sometimes a scholarship is a one-time check. Other school scholarships are renewable and provide money for students each semester or school year. These awards differ from student loans in that they don’t have to be repaid. Students might receive the money directly as a check in their name. In other cases, the money is given to the student’s school. In these cases, the student would then pay the school for the difference on any money owed for tuition, fees, room and board. If the scholarships and other forms of financial aid are enough to cover the direct college costs, the excess money is refunded to the student.

The University provides scholarships to undergraduate and graduate students in the specialty "Information Technology". Based on this scholarship program, the Estonian government annually allocates €700,000 to two universities - Tallinn University of Technology, University of Tartu Institute of Computer Science. Students can apply through universities in an online format.
Requirements
Who can apply for a scholarship?
Undergraduate students majoring in Computer Science and Information Technology;
Master's degree students majoring in Computer Science and Software Development.

Undergraduate Scholarships in Australia

The University of Melbourne offers a scholarship for international students in the Bachelor's degree program. Every year, 50 students from all over the world can get the opportunity to study for free in all specialties offered by the university.
The scholarship can be of three types:
1.$10,000 to pay for tuition in the first year of study at the bachelor's degree program;
2.Payment of 50% of the cost for the entire period of study on the bachelor's degree program;
3.Payment of 100% of the cost for the entire period of study on the bachelor's degree program.

South Korean Government Scholarship Program for International Students

The National Institute of International Education (NIIED) is a responsible administrative agency under the Ministry of Education of Korea. The Institute is working on the internationalization of Korean education in order to develop competitive human resources in the era of globalization.
NIIED works in the following areas:
Assistance in popularization of teaching in foreign languages (exchange programs)
Implementation of educational programs for indigenous Koreans living outside Korea
Attracting and supporting international students
Implementation of the Global Korea Scholarship Program (Global Korean Scholarship)

Anthony Morris Scholarship at Concord College

Organizer: Concord College (UK).
Boys and girls aged 16-17 are invited to participate. List of required documents: Anthony Morris Scholarship Application Form
A personal statement describing the applicant's goals, career/university aspirations, participation in extracurricular activities and participation in community life.
